Childhood

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955 in San Francisco, California. He was adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs as a baby. He grew up in Mountain View, California before moving to Cupertino at 12 years old. It was here that Jobs met Steve Wozniak.

Founding Apple

On April 1, 1967, after Steve Wozniak showed the Apple I to Jobs, Apple Computer was founded.

The Apple I was sold as a motherboard. A screen, keyboard, and any other essential items were added separately.

The Apple II was Steve's second product, also invented by Wozniak. It was released on April 16, 1977.

After the failure of the Macintosh in 1984, Steve Jobs resigned from Apple after the board of directors voted him out.

Return to Apple

After a brief stint at his new company NeXT, Steve re-joined Apple. With Steve's return, Apple was finally seeing success once again.

Working closely with Jonathan Ive, Steve created beautiful products that sold well. His defining products are the iPod and the iPhone.


Death

In 2003, Jobs was diagnosed with a pancreatic tumor. On October 5, 2011, Steve Jobs died at age 56 in Palo Alto, California.
